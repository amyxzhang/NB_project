As discussed in Section 34.2, an important use of diffraction gratings is to
disperse (separate) light into its constituent wavelengths in order to better un-
derstand the light source. The amount of information that can be obtained from
a spectrum depends on whether the wavelengths of interest can be distinguished
from one another in the spectrum (such wavelengths are said to be resolved).
Two wavelengths can be distinguished from each other if the principal maxi-
mum of one falls in the first dark region of the other, as shown in
Figure 34.34.
